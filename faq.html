FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Everything people ask before joining the development updates list. If your question is not here, email szymon.kokot@illucrum.com and we will add it.

Do I have to switch fertilizers to use Ilaequor?

No. Ilaequor works from the fertilizers you already dose. You enter each stock once, the fertilizer, the grams, and the volume of water you dissolved it in, and the app does the dosing math from your tank volume. There is nothing new to buy and no product line to switch to.

Which dosing methods does it support: only GEN, or also EI and PPS-Pro?

The engine is method-agnostic. It tracks levels, ratios, and doses for any fertilizer routine, whether you follow EI (Estimative Index), PPS-Pro, lean dosing, or the GEN ratio method. GEN's N:K and Redfield targets are built in, and you can set your own target ranges for every element.

What tests do I need?

Colour-change reagent tests for ammonium (NH4), ammonia (NH3), nitrite (NO2), nitrate (NO3), and phosphate (PO4), plus a two-reagent titration for calcium and magnesium. Ilaequor times the reagent tests for you and walks the titration through its steps.

How is this different from a dosing calculator like Rotala Butterfly?

A calculator gives you one dose in isolation, from numbers you type in that moment. Ilaequor tracks your tank's measured state over time and recommends the next doses based on where the tank actually sits, so the numbers follow your real tank between tests rather than a generic target.

How is it different from an aquarium log app?

A log records what you type. Ilaequor also models what happens between tests: it dilutes for your water changes and adds what you dosed, then turns your readings into dose recommendations. The whole thing is built around the two-week testing session, not just a diary of past values.

What happens to my data?

Ilaequor stores your tank setup and your readings so it can show history and compute recommendations. You can export or delete your data at any time. Full details are in the privacy policy.
